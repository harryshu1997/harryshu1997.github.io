Frequently Asked Questions

This page addresses common questions I receive from prospective students, collaborators, and the general public. If you don't find your question answered here, please feel free to reach out directly.
For Prospective PhD Students
Are you accepting new PhD students?
Yes, I am actively looking for motivated PhD students to join my research group. I typically admit 2-3 new PhD students each year. Please see the application requirements below and feel free to reach out if you're interested in working together.
What research areas are you most interested in?
My primary research interests include machine learning systems, distributed computing, and AI applications. I'm particularly excited about projects that bridge theory and practice, and that have potential for real-world impact. Check out my projects page for specific examples of current work.
What should I include in my application email?
Please include: (1) A brief introduction of yourself and your background, (2) Your research interests and how they align with my work, (3) Your CV/resume, (4) Any relevant publications or projects, (5) What specifically interests you about working in my group. Please keep the email concise but informative.
Do you accept students without prior ML/systems experience?
Strong mathematical background and programming skills are essential, but I welcome students from diverse academic backgrounds. What matters most is motivation, willingness to learn, and demonstrated ability to tackle challenging problems.
For Current Students
How often do you meet with your students?
I typically meet with each student weekly during active research periods. We also have group meetings bi-weekly where students present their work and get feedback from peers. I maintain an open-door policy for urgent questions.
What are your expectations for PhD students?
I expect students to be proactive, curious, and committed to rigorous research. This includes reading relevant literature, conducting careful experiments, communicating results clearly, and contributing to the research community through publications and open-source software.
For Collaborators & Industry Partners
Are you available for consulting or collaboration?
I'm selective about consulting opportunities but am interested in collaborations that align with my research interests and can benefit my students. Please reach out with specific details about the project and timeline.
Do you give talks at conferences or companies?
Yes, I regularly give invited talks about my research. I'm particularly interested in speaking at venues where I can share our latest work and engage with the community. Please provide details about the event, audience, and timing when reaching out.
General Questions
How did you get into your field?
My path into machine learning and systems research began during my undergraduate studies when I became fascinated by the intersection of mathematics, computer science, and real-world applications. The field has evolved tremendously, and I'm excited to continue contributing to its development.
What advice do you have for students interested in research?
Start early by reading papers, attending seminars, and working on projects. Don't be afraid to reach out to faculty whose work interests you. Build strong fundamentals in mathematics and programming. Most importantly, be persistent and passionate about solving meaningful problems.
